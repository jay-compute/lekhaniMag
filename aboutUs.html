About Us Page

Pranaya S. Ayyala

Co-editor and Founder

Pranaya S. Ayyala is the editor and founder of Lekhani. She was raised in both the United States and India, indulging and identifying with both cultures. Currently, she is pursuing her degree in accounting and minors in creative writing, and has worked as an editorial intern at Lantern Review in the past year. In her free time, she enjoys video games and books - both always accompanied with a warm cup of coffee.

Jevaria Bashir

Reader: Visual Arts

Jevaria Bashir is the visual media reader for Lekhani. She was born in New York and is ethnically Pakistani, from Azad Kashmir. Jevaria is currently pursuing a Computer Science major alongside a certificate for Quantum Computing. Outside of academic pursuits, she enjoys drawing, digital painting, and has done so for 13 years.

Angel Mary Joseph

Outreach/Social Media and Reader: Visual Media

Angel Mary Joseph is part of the outreach team at Lekhani and a visual media reader for the journal. They are a Malayali international student from Kerala, India currently doing Psychology and Film and Media studies with a concentration in Business Studies. They are currently working on minority representation in Bollywood. They are a performance artist and visual arts photographer working under the name Apsara On Film.

Ruchi Hegde

Web Developer

Ruchi Hegde is the web developer for Lekhani. Originally from Philadelphia, but ethnically Kannadiga, Ruchi is a computer science major with a minor in Mandarin Chinese and Business. Along with Lekhani, Ruchi is involved in club swimming, computer science clubs, and other clubs related to her Indian identity. Outside of class, she enjoys working out, painting, and reading.

Anvi Joshi

Reader: Poetry

Anvi Joshi is the reader for poetry at Lekhani. Born in New Jersey, she is ethnically Marathi, from Maharashtra, India. She is currently pursuing a degree in Psychology with minors in Linguistics and Criminal Justice. She enjoys embroidery and yoga in her free time. She has previously won honorable mentions in poetry at the regional Scholastic Arts and Writing Contest, and for Feature Writing at the G.S.S.P.A conference.
